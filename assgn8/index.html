The Triune Brain Model


The biological underpinnings of love in humans.



ABOUT

The Triune Brain model helps demonstrate how the structure and design of the human brain facilitates the experience of love in humans. The Human brain has evolved from three different brains and they now form the highly functional human brain today. These three parts of the brain work together to construct each person’s thoughts and performance in the world.










OVERVIEW



REPTILE

The oldest is the reptilian brain, which is responsible for the neurons that induce vital operations such as breathing, body temperature and heart beat. The reptilian brain comprises of the brainstem and cerebellum, and they show signs of aggression and territorial defense.


MAMMALIAN

The Second part is the limbic system, which first manifested in mammalian brains, and is the center for advanced emotionality. It is also responsible for the enhanced motivation, learning and memory. The limbic brain consists of the hippocampus, amygdala and the hypothalamus. The limbic system can collect sensory information and filter it for emotional relevance. Mammals experience emotions which are motivators that can compel one to take an action or react a certain way.

NEOCORTEX

The most distinct to only a human brain is the Neocortex, which is also the largest and newest of the three brains. Human’s ability for abstract thinking such as language, strategy, planning and problem solving are all conceived here. It is this third largest brain that differentiates the experience of love in humans from other animals.

How do we experience love?


The Laws of attraction illustrates some of the key conventions that attract us to other humans. Love can then blossom from these initial notions that form a connection. One of the conventions described is the “illusion of scarcity” and how “when a person’s availability decreases, their attractiveness increases” (Lehmiller, 180). To be able to perceive and contemplate this rationale, the human brain will use both the limbic system as well as the Neocortex to be able to then strategize and take action. The evolution of the brain now allows humans to be able register abstract knowledge that can play a role in their experience of love. As the limbic system is responsible for emotional memory, this too is a component in the experience of love in humans.

Therefore, the triune model helps us fathom the biological basis for the experience of love in humans. Reptilians would not be able to experience any emotion that a mammal would be able to feel. It is the limbic system and the Neocortex that intensifies the experience with emotional feelings, memories and abstract thinking.
